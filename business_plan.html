5. Key Insights & Strategic Recommendations

  • Sales is the Primary Constraint, not Capacity The founder has ample service bandwidth in Years 1 and 2 (under 50% utilization). All focus and marketing investment must be directed toward **client acquisition** and increasing the **T1 to T2 conversion rate**.
  • Subscription Upsell is Paramount (The 30% Rule) The financial viability hinges on the Tier 1 Audit serving as the primary entry point to secure the Tier 2 Subscription. Hitting the **30% conversion rate** is the single most important KPI for achieving the projected NPV.
  • Proactive Hiring Strategy The hiring plan for Year 3 and 4 should be framed as an **R&D Investment** rather than a capacity necessity, focusing on offloading repetitive tasks to accelerate the high-value SaaS transition.
  • Scenario Planning for Investors The investor deck must clearly present the **Base Case (CHF 124k NPV)**, the Optimistic Case, and the Conservative Case (15% uptake, negative CHF 52k NPV) to demonstrate disciplined risk management.

6.1 Risk and Mitigation Matrix

Risk Category Key Risk Scenario Mitigation Strategy
Technology Evolution Major LLM provider releases native RAG security features. Focus 50% of R&D on non-LLM attack surfaces (vector DB security). Transition to a tool-based SaaS model (Section 7.2).
Client Acquisition Low conversion rate (T1 to T2) due to budget-conscious KMUs. Use T1 audit results as irrefutable evidence of risk, positioning T2 as an essential risk transfer mechanism.
Regulatory Shifts New EU/Swiss AI laws introduce compliance complexity or liability risk. Position the service as a compliance wrapper. Use T2 ARR to fund continuous legal monitoring and framework updates.

7. Growth, Scaling, and Exit Strategy

7.1 Transition to Product (SaaS) and Exit Strategy

The long-term value driver is the underlying automation tool, not just the consulting service. The plan is to migrate the RAG Audit/Monitoring scripts into a self-service SaaS tool by **Year 5+**, reducing Variable OPEX and increasing the valuation multiple.
