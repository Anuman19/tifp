PROBLEM STATEMENT

Project: GenAI Red Teaming for SMEs | Goal: Summarize the central problem formulation

Brief Instructions: The Problem Statement summarizes the central problem formulation in one sentence to develop a common understanding of the problem.

Preparatory Questions (The 5 W's + How)

Who? (Actor)

Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) in high-compliance sectors (e.g., finance, legal) and their IT/Security Managers.

What? (Context)

The security vulnerabilities of their internal and customer-facing Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) GenAI Bots leading to data leakage.

Where? (Location)

In the interfaces (prompts) of the GenAI bots and the underlying RAG database containing sensitive corporate documents.

When? (Timing)

Continuously, but especially after the implementation of new AI bots or updates to the underlying LLM models.

Why? (Reason)

Because the specialized knowledge for LLM Red Teaming is lacking in SMEs, and generic penetration tests do not cover these new vulnerabilities.

How? (Mechanism)

Through Prompt Injection, Model Hacking, or insecure configurations that force the bot to disclose confidential internal data.

Problem Statement (Final Iteration)

How might we help SME decision-makers audit their RAG-powered GenAI bots for Prompt Injection, (re)design, so that the risk of critical data leakage is satisfied, taking into account the limited budgets and lack of internal LLM security expertise ?
